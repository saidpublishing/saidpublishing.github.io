About the Author and Architect of
ËLDAMORIA

Uthman Said is a multifaceted professional with a rich background in engineering, public health advocacy, and community organization. He holds a MASc. and BEng. from Ryerson University, where he excelled academically and took on several leadership roles. His research in reliability engineering led to significant contributions, including the development of failure-prediction algorithms and published maintenance evaluation methodologies.

Uthman’s career spans various impactful roles. As a Community Organizer at Ajyal for Youth Development, he provided crucial support to at-risk youth, refugees, and newcomers, significantly enhancing their integration and well-being. His dedication to social advocacy continued in his role as District Case Manager for Hon. Dr. Hedy Fry, M.P., where he addressed a wide range of constituent issues and advocated for progressive health policies.

In his recent position as Public Health Advocate at the Powell Street Resource Centre, he coordinated essential services for homeless individuals, refugees with mental health issues, and victims of drug addiction, achieving significant operational efficiencies and securing additional funding for the centre. His leadership as Operations Manager at Imam Ghazali Publishing further demonstrated his ability to drive operational improvements and educational outreach.

His journey is marked by his unwavering commitment to social justice, community service, and excellence. His diverse experiences have equipped him with a unique skill set and a deep understanding of the complex challenges facing our communities."
